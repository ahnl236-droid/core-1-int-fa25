How is Perfume Created?

There are certain components necessary to “sandwich” in order to create a fragrance, formulated for most skin types, pleasant-y, and longevity to the person’s first and last spritz.

Application Expertise: Pulse Points

  • Aroma Compounds: The scent itself, from essential oils (distilled) or absolutes (solvent extracted) from natural sources, or synthetic molecules.
  • Solvent/Carrier: Usually high-proof ethanol (perfumer’s alcohol), dissolving oils and aiding diffusion.
  • Fixatives: Slows evaporation for lasting scent, e.g., resins (myrrh, frankincense) or synthetics.
  • Water: Sometimes added to dilute alcohol.
