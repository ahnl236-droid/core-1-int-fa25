How to Spritz Perfume

Depending on your mood and the occasion, you may rack your brain deciding whether to spritz on one of the best woody, gourmand, fresh, fruit-forward, or floral scents. But something that’s often less considered when it comes to your eau de parfum, toilette, or cologne of choice is how to apply it.

Myth: Rubbing Your Wrists

If you typically rub your wrists together immediately after spritzing on a scent, you’re not alone. And the act may not be as taboo as previously suggested. There’s no scientific basis for the idea that rubbing your wrists “crushes” fragrance molecules. However, friction and heat can alter evaporation, causing the scent to fade faster. Let fragrance settle naturally or gently pat instead.

Application Expertise: Pulse Points

  • Wrists (NO rubbing)
  • Neck
  • Behind Ears
  • Inner Elbows
  • Behind Knees & Ankles

Application Expertise: With A Thin Layer

Applying unscented lotion or Vaseline before spritzing can help fragrance adhere to the skin, extending wear time and preserving scent evolution.
