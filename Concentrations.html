The Art of Fragrance

Eau Fraîche

The most diluted fragrance, this concentration provides a delicate scent that makes it ideal for those who prefer to wear concentrations of perfume or to spray for a subtle refresh in a place of closed air.

Fragrance Oil Percentage: 1–3%

Longevity: 1–2 hours

Eau de Cologne

For a delicate aroma that offers with sensitive skin. This concentration is most often used for fragrance brands that have short wear and alcohol content, making it perfect for daily wear, post-shower routine, and warm weather.

Fragrance Oil Percentage: 2–5%

Longevity: 4–6 hours

Eau de Toilette

This is a lighter and fresher concentration of fragrance that is great to wear while lounging or for the daytime office. However a higher alcohol content may not suit a higher lasting scent, which fades the scent faster.

Fragrance Oil Percentage: 5–15%

Longevity: 4–8 hours

Eau de Parfum

Known as the most balanced and lasting scent popular choice of use, this concentration guarantees a lasting scent longer than its lighter concentrations without being overbearing.

Fragrance Oil Percentage: 15–20%

Longevity: 2–3 hours

Extrait de Parfum

This is the highest and richest concentration of pure perfume oils, with perfect execution in projecting scent and the greatest lasting power upon application.

Fragrance Oil Percentage: 20–40%

Longevity: 8–12+ hours
